Learn to prove mathematical statements. Automatically verify it.

Computer verification of mathematical statements and theorems is not novel. But, teaching students to prove maths, and doing this using computer verification, definitely is novel. Usually, writing computer assisted proofs is more difficult than writing out proofs on paper, as these proofs can get very tedious and trivial at times. Waterproof provides a more accessible syntax and powerful tactics to help students learn to prove on a high level, and Waterproofs helps students with the more trivial parts of a proof. Waterproof can verify your proofs in real-time, using Coq, a renowned formal proof management system. Especially for those learning to write proofs, mistakes are bound to happen, and you probably don't have a mathematician nearby to help you at all times.

Novel teaching method. Notebook style.

Notebooks — documents that contain both text and executable code — are an interesting and exciting new way to do development.

Okay, not totally new. Wolfram Mathematica notebooks were introduced in 1988 already. However, creating a mathematical proving environment in a notebook-style UI is novel!
